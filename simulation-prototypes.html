How Light Incident Angle Impacts Solar Panel Power

Measurements paper on how light incidence angle affects power output for consumer-grade photovoltaic panels.

Measurements Solar Energy

Summary

This study quantifies how the angle of incoming light changes the power output of low-cost solar panels. A motorized fixture sweeps the panel through 180 degrees under a fixed LED source, comparing measured power to a projected-area model.

Key ideas

  • Consumer-grade panels show near-sinusoidal power dependence.
  • Deviations likely come from reflection and mounting losses.
  • Sun-tracking could yield near-double total energy.

Excerpt

Results show that the panel’s power output generally follows a sinusoidal dependence expected for ideal photovoltaic cells, with deviations likely due to reflection, scattering, and material losses.

What I learned

  • Fixture design impacts measurement accuracy.
  • Angular uncertainty matters as much as sensor noise.
  • Simple tracking strategies can unlock large gains.
