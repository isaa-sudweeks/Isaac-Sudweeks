Why Simulation Drives Better Prototypes

Reflection on how computational modeling improves iteration speed and reduces material waste during prototyping.

Simulation Prototyping Design

Summary

The essay argues that simulation is not a replacement for physical builds, but a filter that makes each prototype more intentional. I outline the moments when models save time and when they mislead.

Key ideas

  • Use quick models to eliminate weak design concepts early.
  • Validate simulation assumptions with targeted experiments.
  • Pair digital and physical logs to improve iteration speed.

Excerpt

The best simulations do not end the design process. They shorten the path to a prototype that is worth testing in the real world.

What I learned

  • How to balance simulation fidelity with project timelines.
  • Documenting assumptions creates better handoffs.
  • Visual summaries help teams decide faster.
