Wave Cancellation on a Circular Membrane

Journal paper on solving the wave equation with a time-dependent boundary displacement that cancels all membrane motion by time T.

Wave Equation Control Hankel Transform

Summary

This paper applies a finite Hankel transform to a circular membrane wave equation with a Dirichlet boundary condition. We solve for the boundary motion that drives displacement and velocity to zero at a specified final time.

Key ideas

  • Use Hankel-mode decomposition to separate eigenmodes.
  • Solve a non-homogeneous ODE in modal space.
  • Compute a boundary displacement that cancels all modes.

Excerpt

We solved for the boundary displacement so that after time T, all waves on the membrane are canceled and we are left with no motion.

What I learned

  • How boundary control shapes modal dynamics.
  • Transform methods make inverse problems tractable.
  • Clear notation helps readers follow long derivations.
