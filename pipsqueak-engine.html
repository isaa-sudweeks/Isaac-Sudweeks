School Project

Pipsqueak Engine

Built a small air-driven engine using milling, casting, and additive manufacturing to explore precision processes.

Manufacturing Design Prototyping

Overview

The pipsqueak engine project combined multiple fabrication methods to build a working pneumatic engine. It emphasized tolerancing, fit, and process selection.

What I built

  • Machined components using milling and lathe operations.
  • Cast elements with powder metallurgy techniques.
  • 3D-printed fixtures for assembly and alignment.

What I learned

  • Choosing processes based on tolerances and material limits.
  • Iterating designs to improve manufacturability.
  • Balancing precision with available shop resources.
